About

I love learning new things and finding creative solutions to difficult challenges. With interests spanning technology, mathmatics, psychology, design, and more, I have found a career as a front-end engineer has allowed me to satisfy my desire to work with logic and provided an outlet for creativity, while still maintaining a connection with the human element.

After graduating from Baylor University in 2008 with a BA in Philosophy I spent a few years working in the restaurant industry where I worked my way up to a management role. Balancing inventory, food and labor costs, and leading teams of cooks, servers, and support staff through busy shifts was fun and exciting work. I really value the leadership experience I gained during that time. But, eventually it became clear that I needed a change to achieve the life I wanted.

After some careful consideration about my goals, I decided to go back to school and enrolled in Texas State Technical College to earn a degree in Web Development. It was here that I developed a passion for programming. The idea of having a career in which I was able to build things using problem solving skills and logic was perfect.

I was able to land an internship at a marketing agency called Marketing Matters, where I quickly was selected for full time employment as a Jr Web Developer. Marketing Matters catered to clients all the way from a local to an international level. My work there included the support of existing sites, coding for email marketing campaigns, devising SEO strategies, managing Adword campaigns, and building websites from start to finish. As part of a small interactive team, I had a big part in the technical aspects of helping our clients build their digital footprint.

The knowledge and experience I gained at Marketing Matters eventually led me to a job at Kasasa (then called BancVue), building and maintaining websites for small financial institutions across the nation. This was a whole new level of production for me; I was now collaborating closely with an operations team of more than 40 professionals focused on providing a world-class product with ever-shrinking timelines. Though difficult at first, that environment pushed me to learn diligence and focus in coding, and to apply the best practices that are required when working on a team of developers.

My time with Kasasa prepared me for my next career move and current role of senior software engineer at CommScope. Here I build applications for telecom companies such as AT&T; Applications used to monitor and maintain networks ranging from single subscriber homes to nation wide infrastructure. In addition to writing code my responsibilities include mentoring other software engineers, updating technical documentation, working through product testing with QA, communicating with Product Services to understand client needs, helping Product Services integrate software updates, working closely with back-end engineers to define API requirements, and collaborating with UX/UI designers to develop UIs that focus on user needs. As a senior software engineer at CommScope I really enjoy the level of involvement I have thoughout the software development life cycle.
